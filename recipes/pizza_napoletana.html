Pizza napoletana

Description

Pizza Napoletana is likely the most famous pizza style in the whole world. Otherwise known as “Neapolitan pizza,” this style of pizza is easily recognizable by its soft and fragrant dough. The external part of the crust should be fluffy and delicious, and sometimes it’s even stuffed with ricotta cheese to make the whole pizza even tastier.

Ingredients

For the dough

  • Flour - 640g
  • Water - 360g
  • Salt - 14g
  • Yeast - 0.2g to 0.5g (depending on yeast type and temperature)

For the toppings

  • 300g tin of plum tomatoes
  • Salt
  • Mozarella
  • Parmesan
  • Olive oil
  • Basil

Steps

Dough

  1. Mix all the ingredients into a shaggy mass
  2. Cover the bowl with cling film
  3. Leave the dough to rest for 1 hour
  4. Turn the ough out on the counter and knead for around 5 minutes
  5. Place the ough back into the bowl and cover
  6. Divide the dough into 4 equal parts
  7. Place each dough ball into a small bowl and cover
  8. Leave the dough balls to prove again for about 4-6 hours

Sauce

  1. Blend a tin of quality tin tomatoes
  2. Cook the sauce until it reaches the desired thickness
  3. Season with salt and pepper

Cooking

  1. Get your oven temperature up as high as possible, preferably pre-heating a pizza stone (or pizza steel), for one hour
  2. Put a dough ball on your counter top and press from the middle and towards the edges, forming a pizza-shaped dough
  3. Add sauce, then sprinkle a litle bit of salt, before adding basil, parmesan and mozarella
  4. Add olive oil before putting the pizza into the oven
  5. Let cook until the crust develops a leopard pattern and the cheese is golden
  6. Cool off for a few minutes on a cooling rack beofre serving
